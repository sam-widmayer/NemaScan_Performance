CeNDR is a resource maintained by the Andersen Lab which enables the collection and organization of C. elegans wild isolates for quantitative genetics and gene discovery. Recently, we have re-vamped our genome-wide association (GWA) mapping pipeline and expanded set of strains and genetic variants available for exploration. Analyses using simulations are currently ongoing to determine the power and utility of C. elegans for quantitative genetics.

  1. A small overview of mapping performance with a single QTL with a defined effect.
  2. A more nuanced example where there are a small collection of QTLs with effects that are distributed more closely to what we expect to see in nature.
  3. We assessed false-positive rates in this GWA pipeline, with a specific focus on how they can help us conceptualize confidence intervals and divergent regions and different types of variant sets can affect mapping results
  4. C. elegans strains from around the world have vastly different evolutionary histories, shaping genomic variation in different ways. We interrogated how population demography affects GWA performance given a finite sampling capacity.
